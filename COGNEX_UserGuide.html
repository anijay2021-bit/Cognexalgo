3.6 Per-Leg Risk Management

Stop Loss (SL)

Enter a Stop Loss price (absolute LTP level, not P&L) for the leg. When the live LTP of the option hits this level, COGNEX automatically exits the leg.

  • For Sell legs: SL price should be above entry price (option becomes expensive → loss)
  • For Buy legs: SL price should be below entry price (option falls → loss)

Target Price

Enter a Target price. When LTP hits this level in your favor, the leg exits with the profit locked.

  • For Sell legs: Target price is below entry price (option cheapens → profit)
  • For Buy legs: Target price is above entry price (option rises → profit)

Trailing Stop Loss (Trail SL) F1

A trailing SL automatically moves the SL in your favor as the trade moves profitably — locking gains without closing the trade prematurely.

How Trailing SL Works
ScenarioAction TypeBehavior
Option LTP falls from ₹120 → ₹80 Sell (short) Trail SL = ₹20 → Initial SL = ₹140. As LTP falls to ₹80, new SL = ₹100 (80+20). Profit is locked.
Option LTP rises from ₹80 → ₹120 Buy (long) Trail SL = ₹20 → Initial SL = ₹60. As LTP rises to ₹120, new SL = ₹100 (120-20). Profit is locked.

Set Trail SL = 0 in the leg row to disable trailing. Configure the fixed SL level in the Risk Management section below the leg grid.

SL-M Exit Order Type F6

In Live trading mode, enabling Use SL-M on Exit places a Stop-Loss Market order instead of a regular Limit/Market exit order. SL-M guarantees execution at the next available market price once the trigger is hit — useful in fast-moving markets where slippage on a limit SL is a concern.

⚠️
SL-M in Live Mode Only

SL-M orders are only active when the strategy is in Live mode. In Paper mode, a simulated market exit is used regardless of this setting.

3.8 Strategy-Level MTM Risk Management F5

Beyond per-leg stop losses, COGNEX monitors the combined P&L (MTM) of the entire strategy and can exit all legs at once when strategy-level risk limits are hit.

Field (exact UI label)TypeDescription
Trail Distance (₹ or %)₹ numberTrail the strategy's peak MTM P&L. When P&L falls by this amount from peak, exit all legs.
Use % (uncheck = ₹ points)CheckboxIf checked, Trail Distance is treated as a % of peak profit. Uncheck for absolute ₹ points.
Activate at MTM Profit (₹)₹ numberOnce strategy P&L reaches this value, the Lock Profit guard activates.
Lock to MTM Profit (₹)₹ numberMinimum P&L guaranteed after the lock triggers. Strategy exits if P&L falls below this.
Example: Lock ₹10,000 profit once ₹15,000 is reached
  • Set Activate at MTM Profit (₹) = 15000
  • Set Lock to MTM Profit (₹) = 10000

When strategy MTM hits ₹15,000, COGNEX activates a guard: if P&L drops below ₹10,000, all legs are exited immediately. Your ₹10,000 profit is safe.

Example: Strategy-level Trailing SL of ₹5,000
  • Set Trail Distance (₹ or %) = 5000

If peak MTM = ₹20,000 and P&L then falls to ₹15,000 (down ₹5,000 from peak), all legs exit. If instead peak rises to ₹25,000, the new stop becomes ₹20,000.

3.11 Delta-Based Strike Selection F7

The By Delta strike mode uses the live Delta from the GreeksService to select the option strike whose delta most closely matches your target.

How Delta Selection Works
  1. You set Target Delta = 0.25 for a CE (Call) leg.
  2. At entry, COGNEX fetches the live option chain with Greeks.
  3. For every Call option, it reads |Delta| from the GreeksService.
  4. It selects the strike whose |Delta| is closest to 0.25.
  5. That strike is entered as the leg's order.

Common Delta Targets

DeltaPosition TypeTypical Use
0.50ATMShort straddle; maximum time decay
0.30Slightly OTMShort strangle (popular risk/reward)
0.161 std-dev OTMIron condor wings; high probability
0.10Far OTMCheap hedge legs; lottery tickets

Chapter 4 Paper Trading — Simulation Mode

Paper trading lets you run strategies with live market data but no real money. All orders are simulated in software with configurable slippage. Use this to validate your strategy before going live.

4.1 Starting Paper Trade

Steps to Run Your First Paper Trade
  • 1Build your strategy in the Strategy Builder (Chapter 3). Click Save Strategy.
  • 2In the Strategies tab, find your strategy in the Strategies DataGrid. The Mode column shows PAPER (blue) by default.
  • 3Click the ▶ Start button (green) for the strategy row.
  • 4COGNEX begins monitoring live ticks. When the first candle forms (after Start Time = 09:15), it evaluates entry conditions and enters legs.
  • 5Monitor the Legs panel below — each leg shows live LTP, Status (OPEN/PENDING/EXITED), and running P&L.

4.2 Paper Trade Status Indicators

StatusMeaning
PENDINGWaiting for entry conditions to be met
OPENLeg is active, paper position held
EXITEDLeg closed (SL / Target / RMS / Manual)
SQOFFForce-squared off (end-of-day or MaxLoss breach)

4.3 Stopping a Paper Strategy

  • Click the ■ Stop button — all OPEN legs are immediately squared off at current LTP.
  • Final P&L is displayed in the strategy row.

4.4 Switching Between Paper and Live Mode

Each strategy has an independent Paper/Live toggle:

  1. In the Strategies DataGrid, find the Mode column.
  2. Click the toggle button (blue = PAPER, red = LIVE) to switch.
  3. The strategy must be stopped before switching mode.
🚨
Default is Paper Mode — Always

Every new strategy starts in Paper mode by default. You must explicitly switch to Live mode. This prevents accidental live orders during strategy testing.

Chapter 5 Live Trading — Going Live

🚨
Real Money Warning

Live mode places real orders on Angel One. Real funds are at risk. Always paper trade a strategy for at least 5–10 days before going live. Understand all risk parameters before switching to Live mode.

5.1 Pre-Live Checklist

  • ✅ Strategy has run in Paper mode for ≥ 5 days
  • ✅ Paper P&L is positive or within acceptable drawdown
  • ✅ Stop Loss values are set for all legs
  • ✅ Trail Distance (MTM Trailing SL) or Lock Profit configured
  • ✅ Max Profit % and Max Loss % limits set in the Risk section
  • ✅ Product Type = MIS for intraday (auto square-off at 3:20 PM)
  • ✅ Sufficient margin available in your Angel One account

5.2 Enabling Live Mode

  1. Ensure the strategy is Stopped (not running).
  2. Click the PAPER toggle in the Mode column — it turns LIVE.
  3. A confirmation dialog appears. Review and confirm.
  4. Click ▶ Start. COGNEX now places real orders on Angel One.

5.3 Live Order Flow

  1. When entry conditions are met, COGNEX calls the Angel One Order API.
  2. The order appears in your Orders Tab (also visible on Angel One app).
  3. Once filled (COMPLETE), the leg transitions to OPEN status.
  4. On SL/Target hit, COGNEX places an exit order (Market or SL-M if configured).
  5. All events are logged and optionally sent via Telegram.

5.4 Emergency Stop

  • ■ Stop button: Places market exit orders for all OPEN legs immediately.
  • You can also square off manually from your Angel One app if COGNEX is unreachable.
⚠️
Internet Connectivity

If your internet drops while in Live mode, COGNEX cannot monitor positions or place exits. Ensure you have a backup (mobile hotspot). MIS positions are auto-squared by Angel One at 3:20 PM in all cases.

9.3 Best Practices

  • Always set per-leg SL — do not run strategies without any stop protection.
  • Use MIS for intraday — Angel One auto-squares at 3:20 PM as a backstop.
  • Trail SL to lock partial profits — prevents winners from turning into losers.
  • Lock Profit is your safety net — use it when MTM is comfortably in profit.
  • Test adjustment legs in paper first — trigger timing is critical.
  • Use SL-M for high-volatility events — ensures execution during fast moves.

Chapter 10 FAQ & Troubleshooting

Common Questions

Q: My strategy is not entering. What's wrong?

  • Check the Status column — it may show PENDING (waiting for entry conditions).
  • If you have Indicator Entry Conditions, verify the indicator value on your charting app for the configured timeframe. The condition may not yet be met.
  • Ensure the WebSocket is connected (green status bar at bottom).
  • Verify Start Time has passed (default 09:15).

Q: I see REJECTED orders in the Orders tab. How do I fix this?

  • Check the rejection reason in the order row.
  • Common reasons: insufficient margin, order quantity too large, market closed.
  • For margin issues: reduce lot size, or add funds to your Angel One account.

Q: Trailing SL is not moving as expected.

  • Ensure Trail SL field is set to a non-zero value in the leg configuration.
  • For a Sell leg: trailing SL moves DOWN as the option price falls (price falling = profit for you).
  • For a Buy leg: trailing SL moves UP as the option price rises (price rising = profit for you).
  • The initial SL price you set is the starting trailing level — it will only improve, never worsen.

Q: Can I edit a strategy while it is running?

No. Stop the strategy first, edit in Strategy Builder, save, then restart. This prevents inconsistent state between running legs and config changes.

Q: What happens to open positions if the app crashes?

  • In Paper mode: positions are lost (no real orders). The simulation resets.
  • In Live mode: positions remain open at Angel One. COGNEX does NOT have auto-resume on restart. Manually square off from Angel One app or log back into COGNEX and stop the strategy to issue exit orders.

Q: The option chain shows incorrect Delta values.

  • Delta is calculated via Black-Scholes using live LTP, ATM IV, risk-free rate, and time-to-expiry.
  • Ensure the LTP feed is live (check WebSocket status). If disconnected, last-tick prices are stale.
  • Click Refresh Chain to force a refresh.

Q: How do I receive Telegram trade alerts?

  1. Create a bot with @BotFather on Telegram. Copy the BotToken.
  2. Find your Chat ID (message the bot, then visit https://api.telegram.org/bot<TOKEN>/getUpdates).
  3. Edit appsettings.json: set "Enabled": true, paste BotToken and ChatId.
  4. Restart COGNEX. Alerts fire on every signal entry/exit and RMS breach.

Q: How is paper trade P&L calculated?

Paper P&L = (Entry Price − Current LTP) × Lots × Lot Size, with configurable slippage applied at entry. No brokerage deducted in paper mode.
